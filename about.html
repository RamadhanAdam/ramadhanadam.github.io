About

I am a second-year MSc student in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning at PAUSTI (Pan African University Institute for Basic Sciences, Technology and Innovation), Nairobi, supervised by Dr. Albert Njoroge Kahira and Dr. Jude Mwenda. My research sits at the intersection of AI security and representation learning — specifically, detecting novel Windows malware in open-world conditions using self-supervised byte-level embeddings and prototypical few-shot adaptation.

Before this I completed a First Class BSc in Statistics and Programming at Kenyatta University. I have backgrounds in statistics, malware analysis, reverse engineering, bug bounty hunting, and low-resource NLP.

I write the AI From First Principles series on Medium — implementations of foundational models from scratch in C and Python, with emphasis on understanding over copy-paste. I also maintain a reading program working chronologically through 20 landmark AI papers before beginning my thesis.
